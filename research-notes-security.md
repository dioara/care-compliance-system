# Security and Access Control Audit - Research Notes

## CQC Regulation 17: Good Governance & Regulation 12: Safe Care and Treatment

### Key Requirements for Security and Access Control:

**Regulation 17(2)(b)**: Providers must assess, monitor and mitigate risks relating to the health, safety and welfare of service users and others

**Regulation 12**: Providers must do all that is reasonably practicable to mitigate risks to the health and safety of service users

### Security and Access Control Focus Areas:

#### 1. Visitor Management
- **Visitor Log System**: All visitors must sign in and out
- **Identity Verification**: Visitors should show ID where appropriate
- **Purpose of Visit**: Recorded for each visitor
- **Time In/Out**: Accurate records of visitor presence
- **Safeguarding Checks**: Process for managing unknown/unexpected visitors
- **Infection Control**: Visitor screening during outbreaks
- **CQC Inspector Access**: Must always be permitted entry
- **End of Life Visits**: Must be facilitated even during restrictions

#### 2. Door Codes and Access Control
- **Regular Code Changes**: Door entry codes changed regularly (quarterly minimum)
- **Code Distribution**: Record of who has current codes
- **Code Security**: Codes not displayed where visitors can see
- **Keypad Security**: Keypads clean and numbers not worn
- **Staff Access Levels**: Appropriate access for different staff roles
- **Contractor Access**: Supervised or escorted where appropriate
- **Emergency Access**: Systems don't prevent emergency egress

#### 3. CCTV and Surveillance
- **CCTV Policy**: Clear policy on use, storage, and access
- **Privacy Balance**: Cameras positioned to respect dignity and privacy
- **Signage**: Clear signs informing of CCTV use
- **Data Protection**: Compliance with GDPR/Data Protection Act 2018
- **Retention Period**: Footage retained for appropriate period (typically 30 days)
- **Access Requests**: Process for subject access requests
- **Monitoring**: Who monitors and reviews footage
- **Maintenance**: Regular checks that cameras are functioning

#### 4. Missing Person Procedures
- **Missing Person Protocol**: Written procedure in place
- **Risk Assessments**: Individual risk assessments for those who may wander
- **Technology**: Use of door sensors, wander alarms where appropriate
- **Staff Training**: All staff know the missing person procedure
- **Search Protocol**: Immediate search of premises and grounds
- **Police Notification**: When to contact police (immediate for high risk)
- **Family Notification**: When and how to inform family
- **Recording**: All missing person incidents logged and reviewed
- **Preventative Measures**: Environmental adaptations, activities, engagement
- **Debriefing**: Post-incident review and learning

#### 5. Safeguarding and Security
- **Safeguarding Policy**: Robust safeguarding procedures
- **Whistleblowing**: Clear process for staff to raise concerns
- **Preventing Unauthorized Access**: Security measures to prevent intruders
- **Resident Safety**: Balance between security and freedom/independence
- **Least Restrictive Practice**: Security measures proportionate to risk
- **Mental Capacity**: Consideration of MCA/DoLS in security decisions

#### 6. Key Management
- **Key Inventory**: Record of all keys and keyholders
- **Key Security**: Secure storage when not in use
- **Lost Key Protocol**: Procedure for lost/stolen keys
- **Key Return**: Process when staff leave employment
- **Master Keys**: Strict control of master/skeleton keys
- **Medication Keys**: Separate controls for controlled drug keys

#### 7. Personal Belongings Security
- **Valuables Storage**: Secure storage for resident valuables
- **Inventory**: Record of items brought into care home
- **Insurance**: Residents informed about insurance arrangements
- **Lost Property**: Procedure for managing lost items
- **Theft Prevention**: Measures to prevent theft
- **Incident Reporting**: Clear process for reporting missing items

#### 8. Information Security
- **Computer Access**: Password-protected systems
- **Screen Locks**: Automatic screen locks on computers
- **Paper Records**: Secure storage of confidential documents
- **Shredding**: Confidential waste disposed of securely
- **Email Security**: Encrypted email for sensitive information
- **Mobile Devices**: Policy on use of personal devices
- **Data Breaches**: Procedure for managing data breaches

### Audit Questions:

**Visitor Management:**
1. Is there a visitor log system in operation?
2. Are all visitors signing in and out?
3. Is visitor identity verified where appropriate?
4. Are visitors screened for infection control risks?
5. Is there a process for managing unexpected visitors?

**Access Control:**
1. Are door codes changed regularly?
2. Is there a record of who has current codes?
3. Are codes kept secure and not visible to visitors?
4. Do emergency exits function correctly?
5. Are contractors supervised or escorted?

**CCTV:**
1. Is there a CCTV policy in place?
2. Are CCTV signs clearly displayed?
3. Is footage stored securely and GDPR compliant?
4. Are cameras positioned to respect privacy?
5. Is there a process for subject access requests?

**Missing Person Procedures:**
1. Is there a written missing person protocol?
2. Are staff trained in the procedure?
3. Are individual risk assessments in place?
4. Is appropriate technology used (door sensors, etc.)?
5. Are all missing person incidents logged and reviewed?
6. Is police notification timely and appropriate?

**General Security:**
1. Are safeguarding procedures robust?
2. Is there a whistleblowing policy?
3. Are security measures balanced with resident freedom?
4. Is key management effective?
5. Are resident belongings secure?
6. Is information security maintained?

### Common Issues:
- Visitor log incomplete or not used
- Door codes not changed regularly
- Codes visible to visitors
- No CCTV policy or poor GDPR compliance
- Missing person procedure not known by all staff
- Inadequate risk assessments for residents who may wander
- Security measures too restrictive (DoLS implications)
- Poor key management
- Confidential information not secure
- No process for managing lost property

### Best Practice:
- Electronic visitor management system
- Biometric or fob access control
- Regular security audits
- Staff training on security procedures
- Balance between safety and freedom
- Person-centred approach to security
- Technology to support independence (e.g., wander alarms)
- Regular review of security incidents
- Learning from incidents to improve systems
