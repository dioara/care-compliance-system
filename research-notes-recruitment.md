# Recruitment and Induction Audit - Research Notes

## CQC Regulation 19: Fit and Proper Persons Employed

### Key Requirements for Recruitment:

**Regulation 19(1)**: Persons employed must:
- Be of good character
- Have the qualifications, competence, skills and experience necessary for the work
- Be able to properly perform tasks intrinsic to the work (after reasonable adjustments)

**Regulation 19(2)**: Recruitment procedures must be established and operated effectively

**Regulation 19(3)**: Required information must be available (Schedule 3)

### Schedule 3 Information Requirements:
1. **Proof of Identity** including recent photograph
2. **Full Employment History** from compulsory school age, with explanations for gaps
3. **Two Written References** (one from most recent employer)
4. **DBS Check** (Enhanced for care roles)
5. **Proof of Qualifications** relevant to the role
6. **Physical and Mental Health** fitness to perform role
7. **Right to Work in UK** documentation
8. **Professional Registration** (where applicable - NMC, HCPC, Social Work England)

### Recruitment Process Requirements:
1. **Job Description and Person Specification**: Clear role requirements
2. **Application Form**: Standardized format collecting Schedule 3 information
3. **Interview Process**: Structured to assess suitability and competence
4. **Reference Checks**: Must be verified before employment starts
5. **DBS Checks**: Enhanced DBS for all care roles
6. **Qualification Verification**: Certificates checked and copies retained
7. **Right to Work**: Original documents verified
8. **Health Assessment**: Occupational health clearance
9. **Professional Registration**: Checked with regulator (NMC, HCPC, etc.)
10. **Offer Letter and Contract**: Terms and conditions clearly stated

### Induction Requirements (Regulation 18):

**Providers must ensure they have an induction programme that prepares staff for their role**

1. **Care Certificate Standards**: For healthcare assistants and social care support workers
2. **Induction Checklist**: Covering:
   - Organizational policies and procedures
   - Health and safety
   - Safeguarding
   - Infection control
   - Fire safety
   - Data protection and confidentiality
   - Person-centred care
   - Dignity and respect
   - Communication
   - Duty of care
3. **Shadowing Period**: Working alongside experienced staff
4. **Competency Assessment**: Before working unsupervised
5. **Probation Period**: Typically 3-6 months with regular reviews
6. **Supervision**: More frequent during induction (weekly/fortnightly)

### Audit Focus Areas:

**Recruitment:**
1. Are all Schedule 3 checks completed before employment starts?
2. Are DBS checks at the correct level (Enhanced)?
3. Are references verified and satisfactory?
4. Are qualifications checked against original certificates?
5. Are interview records showing assessment of suitability?
6. Are employment gaps explained and explored?
7. Is professional registration current and verified?
8. Are recruitment files complete and well-organized?

**Induction:**
1. Do all new starters complete a structured induction programme?
2. Is the Care Certificate completed for relevant roles?
3. Are induction checklists signed off by both staff and supervisor?
4. Is there evidence of shadowing and supervised practice?
5. Are competency assessments completed before unsupervised work?
6. Are probation reviews conducted at required intervals?
7. Is additional support provided where competency gaps identified?
8. Are induction records complete and accessible?

### Ongoing Monitoring (Regulation 19(5)):
1. **Regular Review**: Fitness of employees must be regularly reviewed
2. **DBS Update Service**: Consider using for ongoing checks
3. **Professional Registration**: Annual verification
4. **Concerns Response**: Robust systems to respond to fitness concerns
5. **Safeguarding Referrals**: Made where appropriate
6. **Professional Body Notifications**: When staff no longer meet requirements

### Common Non-Compliance Issues:
- Employment starting before all checks completed
- Missing or incomplete references
- DBS checks not at Enhanced level
- No verification of qualifications
- Gaps in employment history not explored
- Induction checklists incomplete
- Care Certificate not completed
- Staff working unsupervised before competency assessed
- Poor record keeping
